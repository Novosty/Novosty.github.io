Lamb pilaf

Connoisseurs say that you can try the “correct”, real lamb pilaf only in the countries of Central Asia. Perhaps the fact is that in Soviet times the essence of this dish was reduced to rice porridge with meat, so many of the subtleties of cooking were simply ignored. As a result, such a "simplification" has become widely used in catering establishments. Nowadays, the situation is gradually improving, and modern culinary specialists know that, according to the canons, pilaf should be cooked from lamb, with tail fat, with thinly sliced, not grated carrots, with oriental spices and with crumbly rice. This is the recipe we offer below. Check it out and share your impressions with us!

Preparation:

Prepare rice for pilaf. Pour it into a bowl and rinse thoroughly with cold water, changing it until it becomes clear. Pour very cold water again and leave for at least 1 hour. Cut the lamb for pilaf into small pieces. Peel the onion and cut into thin half rings. Peel the carrots and cut into strips. Peel the heads of garlic from the top husk and roots, leave whole. Heat up the cauldron. Pour in vegetable oil and wait until it is well heated. Then add the onion and sauté until light brown, 10 minutes. Add lamb, fry, stirring, 10 minutes. Put carrots in a cauldron, sprinkle with cumin and cook for another 15 minutes. Pour two cups of hot water into the prepared pilaf, salt well and bring to a boil. Then reduce the fire to a minimum. Cook pilaf with a light boil for 40 minutes. Increase the fire under the cauldron, put in it a pod of pepper and garlic. Rinse the rice again and drain in a colander. Place the rice in the pot and level the surface. Gently pour about 800 ml of boiling water along the wall of the cauldron (the water should be 2 cm above the level of the rice). Close the lid and bring to a boil as quickly as possible. When the pilaf boils, remove the lid and reduce the heat to medium. Wait for the water to leave the surface of the pilaf and reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a lid and cook for another 20 minutes. Check the readiness of rice in pilaf. Gently loosen the rice with the handle of a long spoon, remove the hot pepper and garlic. Transfer the lamb pilaf to a large plate and stir to combine. Garnish with garlic and pepper.
