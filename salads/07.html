Pilaf with beef, cranberries and nuts

Pilaf with beef, cranberries and nuts - an unexpected combination, right? Meanwhile, it is quite classic, as these products are combined with each other and complement the taste of each. Moreover, a similar dish called pulao is also prepared in India. And in Uzbek or Azerbaijani cuisine, among hundreds of similar recipes, there will be more than one. Remember the main rule of oriental food: rice for it should be long-grain (the variety is not important). We mix it with wild in equal proportions. The second condition: the main ingredients are prepared separately and almost at the end are combined in one container. We add vegetables and herbs, do not save on bright spices - turmeric, paprika, coriander and black pepper - the gastronomic pearl of the East is ready!

Ingredients

  • beef400 g
  • long grain and wild rice mix250 g
  • bulb1
  • carrot1
  • dried cranberries50 g
  • walnuts50 g
  • sprigs of parsley3
  • sprigs thyme3-4
  • bay leaf1
  • ground turmeric1 tsp
  • ground paprika½ tsp
  • ground coriander½ tsp
  • vegetable oil5 st. l.
  • saltby taste
  • freshly ground black pepperby taste

Step-by-step cooking recipe:

Step 1

Cut the meat into narrow strips, onions into small cubes, carrots into thin circles. Chop the parsley, separate the thyme leaves from the stems.

Step 2

Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan. Simmer onion over medium heat until translucent, 4 minutes. Add carrots, turmeric, paprika and coriander and cook for 5 more minutes. Put the meat and fry, stirring constantly, 5-6 minutes.

Step 3

Pour the rice into the pan, season with salt and pepper and add the bay leaf. Pour in 400 ml of hot water, mix gently and cook over low heat under the lid for 20 minutes. Put the cranberries, nuts, thyme and parsley into the pilaf and continue to cook for another 5 minutes. Remove the bay leaf, remove the pilaf from the heat and leave covered for another 5 minutes.
