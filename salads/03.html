Tashkent plov

Tashkent plov was once cooked in the capital of Uzbekistan only on major holidays. Today, it is replacing the teahouse, which until recently was considered the most popular type of this dish, everywhere. How is one different from the other? First of all, the fact that Tashkent pilaf contains chickpeas, which makes the texture of pilaf denser and the taste richer. Bones are not put in the "capital" version, but fat tail fat is added in excess. As a result, the dish turns out to be unusually fragrant and dizzyingly appetizing. Will you get one like this? We are sure yes! Moreover, if you and, for example, your friend cook according to the same recipe, you will get two different tastes, but equally wonderfully delicious Tashkent pilaf. Why is that? Yes, because, according to experts, water, air, the quality of the ingredients and even the mood of the cook. No need to compare and analyze: just try and get a vivid gastronomic experience!

Ingredients

  • lamb pulp from the sternum or from the loin1-1.2 kg
  • tail fat300 g
  • carrots1 kg

  • heads of garlic2-3 pcs.
  • rice varieties laser1 kg
  • raisins kishmish100 g

Step-by-step cooking recipe:

Step 1

Prepare chickpeas for Tashkent pilaf. To do this, pour it into a saucepan and fill it with plenty of water. Leave for 24 hours. Drain the chickpeas in a colander and rinse under running water.

Step 2

Cut the lamb pulp for pilaf into 8-10 pieces. Measure 100 g of tail fat and cut into cubes with a side of 1 cm. Chop the carrots into thin strips 5–6 cm long. Cut the onion into half rings. At the heads of garlic, cut off the root part and remove the upper layers of the husk, leaving only the thinnest.

Step 3

Prepare rice for Tashkent pilaf. Rinse it seven to eight times in plenty of water until it runs clear. Then cover the rice with warm water (37°C) and generously salt and leave for 1 hour. Kishmish pour a small amount of hot water.

Step 4

Prepare zirvak for pilaf. Heat the oil in a cauldron over high heat until a white haze appears. Dip the diced fat and a large piece of fat into it. Fry until golden brown. Do not mix small cubes of fat. Turn a large piece over so that it is fried on all sides. Lay out on a plate with a slotted spoon.

Step 5

Put the lamb in a cauldron with boiling fat and fry until the liquid has evaporated and the pieces are golden brown. Then add the onion and fry until light yellow. Level the meat with onions, spread the carrots on top.

Step 6

At the stage of preparing pilaf “first water”, pour 1.25 liters of hot water into the cauldron. Press the carrots with a slotted spoon and immerse the heads of garlic and the pod of chili pepper into the future zirvak. Add sugar and all spices, rubbing the cumin with your fingers.

Step 7

Spread chickpeas on top of carrots and spread evenly. Simmer uncovered for 40-60 minutes. In the middle of the process, remove the heads of garlic and pepper, set aside. Immerse a fried piece of fat in zirvak. Simmer until most of the water has evaporated so that you can safely lay a layer of rice on top of the zirvak. At the end, salt heavily (more than necessary).

Step 8

Drain all the water from the pilaf rice. Put the rice on the zirvak and smooth it out. Pour the hot "second water" through the slotted spoon so that the water does not "beat" the rice layer. Add it so much that it barely reaches the top grains. Flatten the rice again and season with a little salt.

Step 9

Increase the fire to the maximum. Zirvak and water should boil evenly and give powerful steam that will process the rice. Then he will "take" the liquid as much as necessary. The water should evaporate to a layer of zirvak.

Step 10

Close" pilaf. Taste the rice first: it should be dry, but almost cooked (the inside is not crunchy). Carefully, with a slotted spoon, collect the rice in a hemisphere from the walls to the center. Sprinkle with raisins, stick chili peppers on top. With a thin stick, make several holes to the bottom of the cauldron.

Step 11

Pour a teaspoon of hot water into the holes. Level the surface. Close the cauldron tightly with a lid and reduce the heat to a minimum. Boil 30 minutes. Remove fat tail fat along with lamb pulp. Cut the meat into small pieces, and the fat into thin plates.

Step 12

Put rice, carrots, chickpeas and raisins on a large flat dish (lyagan). Spread the meat on top. Along the edge of the dish, lay the petals of fat tail fat directly on the Tashkent pilaf. Place garlic cloves and peppers on top.
