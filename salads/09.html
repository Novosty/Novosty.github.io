Pilaf with dried fruits and pumpkin seeds

Pilaf with dried fruits is not a dessert, but a full-fledged second, which is suitable for vegetarians, fasting people and those who love variety in food. The main rule of this oriental dish is that it should be crumbly. This means that rice for it you need to choose certain varieties - basmati, jasmine, devzira (Central Asian version), valencia, indica or "regular" long grain. Any rice presented goes well with white and dark raisins, dried apricots or apricots, prunes and dates, as well as dried cherries or cranberries, nuts and pumpkin seeds. Bring its taste to perfection by adding bright spices - turmeric, sweet paprika and dried mint. By the way, pilaf (another name for the dish) can be made from other cereals - bulgur and other wheat cereals without steam processing or exotic wild cargo rice. The latter has a large amount of nutrients (especially proteins) and fiber and has a sweetish nutty taste - what you need for such a pilaf.

Ingredients

  • basmati rice200 g
  • dark raisins1 st. l.
  • light raisins1 st. l.
  • dried apricots4 pcs.
  • prunes4 things.
  • pumpkin seeds50 g
  • ground turmeric1 tsp
  • sweet paprika0.25 tsp
  • dried mint0.5 tsp
  • vegetable oil4 tbsp. l.
  • sprigs of mint2
  • saltby taste

Step-by-step cooking recipe:

Step 1

Scald dried fruits with boiling water and pat dry with paper towels. Cut dried apricots and prunes into slices.

Step 2

Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan and add dried fruits and pumpkin seeds. Season with paprika, dried mint and turmeric and saute, 3 minutes. Pour rice into the pan, salt and pour in 350 ml of hot water. Cook covered over low heat for 15 minutes. Stir gently several times.

Step 3

Divide the plov into bowls and sprinkle with chopped mint.
