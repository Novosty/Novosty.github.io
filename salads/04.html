Sweet plov with dried apricots, raisins, cinnamon and powdered sugar Shirin

Not a single feast in Azerbaijan can do without this pilaf. It must be served at all celebrations and events. "Shirin" pilaf means "sweet" pilaf and is usually served with sweet dried fruits.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice300 years
  • Saffron 1 g
  • Ghee 150 g
  • White raisins100 g
  • Prunes 80 g
  • Weight 80 g
  • Dried plum “albukhara”100 g
  • Dates 120 g
  • Powdered sugar100 g
  • Cinnamon 10 g
  • Saltby taste

Step-by-step cooking recipe:

Step 1

Boil rice in salted water until half cooked. Throw away in a colander.

Step 2

Prepare saffron tincture. 1 g of saffron pour 200 ml of warm water and let it brew. For pilaf use 2 tbsp. l.

Step 3

Heat the cauldron, grease the bottom with melted butter. Knead the elastic and soft dough, roll it into a thin circle and cover the bottom of the cauldron with it. The dough will not allow the rice to burn and will become one of its favorite parts (pilaf) during cooking - a fragrant fried crust.

Step 4

Lay the rice on top of the dough. Drizzle with melted butter and 2 tablespoons of saffron tincture. Close with a cotton towel and lid. Cook over low heat for 30-40 minutes.

Step 5

Rinse dried fruits, soak in warm water until swollen. Lightly fry in melted butter in a preheated pan for 5-7 minutes.

Step 6

Put the resulting rice on a plate. Serve cooked dried fruits and a mixture of powdered sugar and cinnamon next.
